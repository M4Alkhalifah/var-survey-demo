What happened: Lampard's shot bounced ~60 cm over the goal line, then spun back out. The referee missed it. England lost 4–1. Two years later, FIFA approved Goal-Line Technology because of moments exactly like this.
Hawk-Eye Goal-Line Detection

What modern AI would say

Hawk-Eye GLT deployed since 2012
GOAL CONFIRMED

X-VARS explanation engine

"The ball crossed the goal line in full. Award the goal. The defender's recovery does not change the fact that the ball was already in the goal." — in the style of Held et al. 2024.

Maradona's "Hand of God" — Argentina vs England

What happened: Maradona punched the ball into the net with his hand. The referee did not see it and gave the goal. A perfect retroactive test case for modern AI handball detection.
3D Pose Estimation · Body-Part Detection
SoccerNet-v3D triangulates body keypoints from broadcast replays; the wrist is the contact point, head is 30 cm below.

What modern AI would say

SoccerNet-v3D 3D pose triangulation
HANDBALL DETECTED

3D pose detection shows the ball was hit by the left wrist, not the head. The hand was about 30 cm above the head.

X-VARS explanation engine

"The ball was struck with the player's hand, not his head. The arm was raised above the head and made contact with the ball. Goal must be disallowed and a free kick awarded to the defending team." — in the style of Held et al. 2024.

Pepe's Stamp on Casquero — A Foul Recognition Case

What happened: Pepe (Real Madrid) stamped on Casquero (Getafe) inside the penalty area, then went after a second Getafe player. He received a 10-match ban — one of the longest in La Liga history. A textbook reckless red-card moment for AI foul detection.
Multi-View Foul Classification · Detection Boxes
VARS encodes 4 synchronised camera views; bounding boxes locate players, vectors estimate velocity, contact zone is highlighted.

What modern AI would say

VARS (Held 2024) multi-view foul classifier
RECKLESS · RED CARD

Classified as a high-severity tackle using 4 synchronised camera views. Recommended sanction: red card.

Foul-Pose (Goka 2025) pose-based predictor

"Detected the dangerous tackle 0.8 seconds before contact, by reading the player's body posture." — pre-emptive prediction from pose.

5
Tifo Football · Explainer

Why Tight Offsides Are Hard — The Millimetre Problem

YouTube
Click to play ►
Trouble loading? Open on YouTube ↗
What happened: Modern offside checks measure to the millimetre. A player can be offside by his shoulder, armpit, or even his nose. This Tifo Football explainer shows how SAOT replaces 70-second VAR checks with automatic 25-second decisions, using 12 cameras and 29 body points per player.
Body-Part Offside Detection · Side View
Pose models locate every keypoint (shoulder, hip, knee). The "offside line" is the most-forward defender body-part; the attacker is offside by the most-forward part of their own body.

What modern AI would say

Real-Time Offside (Desai 2025) single-camera
OFFSIDE · 0.7 cm

Decides offside in under 1 second using a single broadcast camera. No 12-camera setup needed.

AutoExplain (Rahimian 2025) natural-language reasoning

"At the moment of the pass, the attacker's leading shoulder is 0.7 cm beyond the second-last defender. Technically offside, but below the threshold of human visual perception." — the kind of explanation referees and fans need.

Time to decision SAOT vs old VAR
25 s

SAOT cuts decision time from ~70 s (VAR) to ~25 s.

Offside · Body-part precision
Cited methods: Real-Time Offside (Desai) · AutoExplain (Rahimian)
